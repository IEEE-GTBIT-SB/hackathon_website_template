The first production line was made in the 20th century and is still widely used, living in the 21st century, don't you think we should build something more apt for our generation.

Special Tracks

Cybersecurity

Cybersecurity is the defense against cyberthreats for systems connected to the internet, including their data, software, and hardware. Organizations use various techniques to prevent unauthorized access to data centers and other computerized systems. Risk management along with decision-makers who identify opportunities that go beyond preventing data breaches may begin to realize the full potential and benefits that cyber security can provide.

Edtech

Edtech is the integration of education, technology, and business. It refers to technological implementations in the classroom. In the pandemic era Edtech has become the new frontier of education.The ability to engage students is one of educational technology's greatest gifts, and thanks to its dynamic nature, it may completely alter how pupils learn in the classroom. Can you use the newest technologies to take on the toughest difficulties in education?

Agritech

By 2050, the global population is expected to increase by 30%, meaning there will be around 10 billion people to feed. The answer to the needs of modern agriculture is agritech, which blends agriculture with technology. From automated irrigation and software for predicting pests, to improved production - agritech is moving farming into the future. Get into agritech and leave a mark from the inside out.

Fintech

Fintech is short for financial technology. It refers to the use of technology to streamline and speed up financial services and transactions. FinTech businesses no longer offer "one size fits all" fixes. Instead, they offer specialized services that cater to a particular financial need, frequently for a lot less money than traditional financial service providers.

Details about the Rounds:

Round 1: This will be a shortlisting round. Each team has to present an abstract of their ideas on any one theme. You can make the submission in the form of ppt, website, application, pdf, model, etc. The brief should be as well explained as possible. The top 10 teams qualify for the next round.

Round 2: The teams selected for Round 2 are required to present their ideas offline at GTBIT. Those who cannot appear offline can also present online through google meet. The proposal will contain the executive summary, need analysis/secondary survey, the scope of the idea, step-wise implementation plan, impact analysis of the idea, timeline for execution, and references. The top 3 teams qualify for the final round.

Final Round: In the final round, the teams selected for the final round will be invited to GTBIT, Delhi on 3rd September 2022. The selected teams will then present their solution in front of the esteemed panel/ judges.

The top entries will win cash prizes as well as certificates.

Rules and Regulations:

  • You can register only in a team of 2 to 4 members.
  • The competition will be conducted in three rounds.
  • Inter-college teams are permitted.
  • The entries will be judged on the basis of Logical Flow, Analytical Reasoning, Innovative solutions, and presentation.
  • All the participating teams will receive a certificate of participation.
  • Only the submissions done within the deadline will be considered for the competition.
  • The teams selected for the final round will have to acknowledge their presence.

Timeline for The Competition:

  • Round 1: 02 Sept 22, 06:00 PM IST - 03 Sep 22, 12:00 AM IST (All the submissions should be done by the deadline)
  • Round 2: 03 Sep 22, 10:00 AM IST - 03 Sep 22, 01:00 PM IST (All the submissions should be done by the deadline)
  • Final Round: 03 Sep 22, 02:00 PM IST - 03 Sep 22, 04:00 PM IST

For the last two rounds, the teams have to report at GTBIT, New Delhi by 9:45 AM. In case the team can't report offline, kindly join online, the link of which will be provided a day prior to the competition.
